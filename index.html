Brief Description and Relevance of the Course

To maximize performance and profitability, businesses should manage their production capacity, resources, and product availability policies across a variety of selling channels. This is what Pricing and Revenue Management (PRM) focuses on. Selling the right product to the right consumer at the right time for the right price has been clearly stated as the main objective of revenue management. Companies supplying seasonal or perishable goods in unstable or shifting markets can benefit greatly from PRM.

Industries Using PRM

Travel

Airlines, trains, rental cars

Hospitality

Hotels, cruise lines, casinos

Retail

Clothing, consumer products

Healthcare

Clinics and hospitals

Financial Services

Banking and insurance

Manufacturing

Made-to-order production

The theory and application of revenue management and pricing are both introduced in this course. Fundamentally, revenue management is an applied discipline, and the success it has in the business world is what gives it value. The technical aspects of the topic merit careful consideration, and it also includes strong characteristics of applied science. This course aims to cover both these practical and theoretical components.

Appendices

Appendix A: Revenue Optimization Case Study Analysis

Objective: To apply theoretical knowledge of pricing and revenue management to a real-world business scenario.

Instructions

Students will be provided with a case study focusing on a company facing revenue optimization challenges. They are required to analyze the case, identify key problems, propose data-driven solutions using PRM techniques, and justify their recommendations.

Deliverables:

  • Written Report (2000-2500 words): Detailed analysis, proposed solutions, and justification.
  • Presentation (15 minutes): Summary of findings and recommendations to the class.

Submission Deadline: As announced by the instructor.

Assessment Criteria:

  1. Depth of analysis and problem identification (20%)
  2. Relevance and creativity of proposed PRM solutions (20%).
  3. Justification of recommendations with quantitative and qualitative arguments (30%)
  4. Clarity and coherence of the written report and presentation (20%)
  5. Adherence to academic integrity (10 %).

Appendix B: Dynamic Pricing Project

Objective: To gain practical experience in implementing and evaluating dynamic pricing strategies in a simulated market environment.

Instructions

Students will work in teams to participate in a dynamic pricing simulation. Each team will make pricing decisions for a product or service over several simulated periods, aiming to maximize revenue. Teams will analyze market data, adjust pricing algorithms, and adapt to competitive actions.

Deliverables:

  • Simulation Performance Report: Documenting pricing decisions, outcomes, and lessons learned.
  • Team Presentation: Discussing strategy, challenges, and successes in the simulation.

Submission Deadline: As announced by the instructor.

Assessment Criteria:

  1. Effectiveness of pricing strategies in maximizing revenue (30%).
  2. Analytical approach to market data and competitive response (30%).
  3. Team collaboration and decision-making process (25%).
  4. Quality of the performance report and presentation (15%)

Appendix C: Pricing Strategy Reflection Essay

Objective: To encourage critical reflection on the ethical and societal implications of various pricing strategies.

Instructions

Students will write a reflective essay on a chosen pricing strategy (e.g., surge pricing, predatory pricing, psychological pricing), discussing its ethical considerations, impact on consumers and society, and potential regulatory challenges. They should draw upon course concepts and external research.

Deliverables:

  • Reflective Essay (1000-1200 words): Individual submission.

Submission Deadline: As announced by the instructor.

Assessment Criteria:

  1. Depth of ethical analysis and reflection (25%).
  2. Integration of course concepts and external examples (25%).
  3. Clarity of arguments and coherence of writing (20%).
  4. Originality of thought and critical perspective (30%).

Appendix D: Revenue Management Tools & Technologies Research

Objective: To explore current and emerging technologies and tools used in pricing and revenue management across different industries.

Instructions

Students will individually research and present on a specific revenue management software, analytical tools, or technological innovation (e.g., AI in pricing, blockchain for revenue sharing). The presentation should cover its functionalities, industry applications, benefits, and limitations.

Deliverables:

  • Research Paper (1500 words): Documenting findings.
  • Presentation (10 minutes): Key insights and demonstrations (if applicable).

Submission Deadline: As announced by the instructor.

Assessment Criteria:

  1. Thoroughness of research and understanding of the chosen tool/technology (20%).
  2. Relevance of industry applications and examples (20%).
  3. Clarity and organization of the research paper and presentation (20%).
  4. Critical evaluation of benefits and limitations (20%).
  5. References to credible sources (20%).
